# GRAAVITONS Student Management System (SMS)
## Business Functionality & Analytics Value Document

**Prepared for:** Client Presentation  
**Date:** 2 April 2026

---

## 1) Executive Summary

GRAAVITONS SMS is an end-to-end academic operations platform designed for coaching institutes and schools to manage students, batches, exams, performance tracking, and outcome improvement in one system.

The platform combines:
- **Operational management** (student records, batch administration, exam workflows)
- **Performance intelligence** (subject, student, and batch analytics)
- **Intervention tools** (risk identification, weak-topic diagnostics, feedback tracking)

This allows leadership and faculty teams to move from manual administration to **data-backed academic decision making**.

---

## 2) Core Business Functionalities

## 2.1 Secure User Access & Roles
- Role-based login and user management (e.g., Admin/Teacher workflows)
- Protected API access and authenticated sessions
- Controlled access to operational and analytics modules

**Business value:** Improves data confidentiality, accountability, and governance across teams.

## 2.2 Batch Lifecycle Management
- Create, list, rename, and manage academic batches
- Organize by academic year, program type, and subject configurations
- Batch-level controls for student and exam workflows

**Business value:** Standardizes operations across multiple cohorts and reduces administrative overhead.

## 2.3 Student Information Management
- Single student profile with academic and personal details
- Parent/guardian information management
- Historical education records (10th/12th), entrance exam details, counselling data
- Student-level update and maintenance workflows

**Business value:** Provides a unified student intelligence record for better counseling and planning.

## 2.4 Student Onboarding at Scale
- Individual student creation
- Bulk upload through Excel templates
- Bulk update/edit capabilities via structured sheets

**Business value:** Speeds up admissions-cycle data entry and minimizes manual errors.

## 2.5 Exam & Marks Management
- Unit test entry and monthly test entry
- Flexible support for numeric and non-numeric mark entries (e.g., absences)
- Subject-level and total-level marks normalization logic
- Date-based and batch-based exam organization

**Business value:** Ensures complete and accurate assessment capture across varying exam formats.

## 2.6 Report & Export Capabilities
- Batch performance report generation
- Excel-based structured exports for stakeholders and audits
- Template-driven data capture and sharing

**Business value:** Improves transparency, reporting speed, and communication with management.

## 2.7 Achiever Showcase Management
- Add, view, edit, and remove achiever records
- Dashboard-ready achievement presentation

**Business value:** Enables institutional branding, recognition culture, and motivation reinforcement.

## 3) Analytics Capabilities (Business Explanation)

The analytics layer is designed to support **academic quality improvement** and **timely intervention**.

## 3.1 Subject-wise Analysis
- Compares student performance by subject
- Tracks average, top, and low outcomes over time
- Works with filter options such as grade, batch, date range, and subject

**What this tells leadership:**
- Which subjects are consistently strong/weak
- Where faculty support or syllabus pacing changes are needed

## 3.2 Branch-wise Analysis
- Compares performance patterns across branches/sections
- Highlights variance between learning groups

**What this tells leadership:**
- Which branches are overperforming/underperforming
- Where teaching process standardization is required

## 3.3 Individual Student Analysis
- Consolidated student-level performance view
- Unit and monthly progression visibility
- Integrated feedback history

**What this tells faculty/counselors:**
- Whether a student is improving, stable, or declining
- What kind of personalized support is needed

## 3.4 Batch Performance Dashboard
- Trend analytics (date-wise progression)
- Subject breakdown (unit + monthly)
- Top and bottom performer lists
- Score distribution views and participation indicators

**What this tells management:**
- Overall batch health
- Whether performance improvement initiatives are working

## 3.5 Advanced Batch Analytics
- Median and quartile analytics
- Percentile bands
- Variability and outlier identification
- Difficulty index by test/unit

**What this tells decision-makers:**
- Spread of performance, not just average
- Whether gaps are widening or narrowing
- Which tests/topics are unusually difficult

## 3.6 Student Metrics & Risk Scoring
- Trend slope (direction of performance)
- Participation rate in conducted tests
- Consistency and non-numeric mark patterns
- Composite risk level with recommended action

**What this tells academic heads:**
- Which students require immediate intervention
- Which students need monitoring vs. regular mentoring

## 3.7 Weak Topic Diagnostics
- Unit-level weak areas for each student
- Difficulty indicator and remediation suggestions

**What this tells teachers:**
- Exactly where to focus revision/remedial sessions
- How to prioritize classroom time for impact

## 3.8 Subject Diagnostics at Batch Level
- Unit-wise average performance
- Weak student segments
- Improving student segments (trend-based)

**What this tells program owners:**
- Topic mastery status at cohort level
- Which intervention programs are delivering results

## 4) Business Benefits of the Analytics Layer

## 4.1 Early Warning & Proactive Intervention
Risk ranking and trend detection help identify at-risk students before major exams.

**Benefit:** Lower failure risk and better retention/performance outcomes.

## 4.2 Better Academic Planning
Subject and unit diagnostics guide focused teaching plans.

**Benefit:** Higher teaching effectiveness and optimized lesson planning.

## 4.3 Data-Driven Faculty Decisions
Branch and batch comparisons provide objective evidence for coaching strategies.

**Benefit:** Reduced dependency on intuition; improved consistency in outcomes.

## 4.4 Higher Parent Communication Quality
Feedback history and structured progress insights enable meaningful parent interactions.

**Benefit:** Better trust, clearer expectations, and stronger parent engagement.

## 4.5 Operational Efficiency
Bulk imports, standardized templates, and reusable reports reduce repetitive admin tasks.

**Benefit:** Saves staff time and improves process reliability.

## 4.6 Institutional Reputation & Growth
Achiever tracking plus measurable outcomes supports branding and admissions positioning.

**Benefit:** Stronger market credibility and potential enrollment growth.
